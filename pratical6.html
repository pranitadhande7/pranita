Tribute to Swami Vivekananda

Introduction

Swami Vivekananda was an influential spiritual leader and reformer from India.

Early Life:

    Birth Name: Narendranath Datta.
  • Date of Birth: January 12, 1863, in Kolkata, India.
  • Family: He was born into an upper-middle-class family. His father was a lawyer, and his mother was a deeply religious woman.
  • Education: Vivekananda studied at the Presidency College in Kolkata, where he was introduced to Western philosophy and the ideas of great thinkers such as Kant, Hegel, and others. He was particularly drawn to spiritual and philosophical subjects.

    • Death:

    Swami Vivekananda passed away on July 15, 1902, at the age of 39. Despite his short life, his teachings and legacy continue to inspire millions of people worldwide. Vivekananda's philosophy continues to be a guiding light for many individuals seeking spiritual enlightenment, self-realization, and the betterment of society.

Famous Quote

"Arise, awake, and stop not until the goal is reached."

Key Teachings

  • Self-realization
  • Helping others
  • Religious harmony
